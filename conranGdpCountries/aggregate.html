Aggregate Health Expenditure by Country, USD (2011-2015)

The United States exceedingly spends more on healthcare over other nations. While spending cannot infer the quality of care, a seemingly positive trend suggests the amount spent on health will continue to increase.

Aggregate Military Expenditure by Country, USD (2011-2015)

As expected the United States spends the most in aggregate on defense. Between 2011-2015, however the amount of spending has slowly decreased for the US. China who lags in second, displays a sharp increase in military expenditure. Russia, another military power lies in third, but not far off from NATO countries.

Aggregate Government Education Expenditure by Country, USD (2011-2015)

World Bank does not provide an aggregate expenditure for education, but does capture the government's spending on education. For this dataset, missing values were imputed by the average of the remaining data points by country. China, who does not provide any data on education spending, is reflected as having zero spending on this sector. Reviewing the expenditures, the United States surprisingly spends the most on education of any country by far. Further research could better understand if this relates to higher education and research or ineffeciencies in early education.
