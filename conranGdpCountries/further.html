How the Top 11 Nations Spend

Further levels of analysis for the
Top 11 Nations Ranked by GDP (2011-2015)

Removing GDP from the analysis allows a closer inspection of expenditures by sector, in aggregate and per capita. The overall change of each country between 2011 and 2015 is also reviewed in aggregate and by percent. Finally, the United States' expenditures are considered independently.

Average Expenditure, Aggregate USD$ (2011-2015)

With a better relation among the data, a better understanding of expenditures within and between nations is realized. In aggregate, the US spends exceeds the spending of all other nations in military, education, and healthcare. Both the United States and China seem to spend a disproportionate amount on healthcare with all other nations seeming to divest similarly into these sectors. Please note China is incorrectly categorized due to missing education data.

Average Expenditure, Per Capita USD$ (2011-2015)

Dividing by population, reduces the United States to second in spending for these three sectors. Canada leads despite its minimal investment in military. The US still spends an exceeding amount on healthcare but is education spending is proportional to other nations. China is missing education data as before.

Overall Change in Expenditure for Health and Education, Aggregate USD$ (2011-2015)

Reviewing the average change in spending for health and education, only the US, China, UK and India saw increases in health spending. For education only the United States and the United Kingdom increased spending.

Overall Change in Expenditure for Health and Education, Percent (2011-2015)

Using percent change, a better comparison of the data is revealed and health spending is revealed to have a large overall change. The UK, US,and India saw positive spending in both sectors (China does not report education). All other nations experienced largely negative growth in spending.

United States Expenditures by Sector as a Function of GDP

Finally, as the United States maintains the largest GDP and is the largest aggregate spender, a brief pie chart was created to display how much of American GDP is spent on the Health, Education, and Military sectors. Together, these sectors account for about 25% of American GDP.
