Concluding Remarks

Reviewing the top 11 countries by GDP in health, education, and military expenditures both by aggregate and per capita paints a narrative between developed Western nations and late-developing countries including China and India, and to a lesser extent Russia. Overall, the United States remains the top nation both by aggregate and per capita. While its defense and education spending are justified and somewhat proportional to other developed nations, its healthcare spending is a magnitude above any other nation which signals either that health is a priority or this sector is not efficient. China is also revealed to have a reduced status when its GDP and spending are considered on a per capita basis. This signals that while the aggregate growth might suggest a rising power, the the total population has yet to be leveraged to further improve and benefit from this growth.

Limitations

This mode of analysis, using HTML, CSS, and Google API, is a new endeavor for Conran Economics. As such, there is much room to improve this level of analysis. Beyond this limitation, the five year span and focus on the top 10/11 nations provides a limited scope to understand trends and relationships between GDP and spending. Finally, missing data in education and years 2016 and 2017 reduce the overall validy of this report.
